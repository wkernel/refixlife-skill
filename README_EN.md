# Skills: Fix Your Entire Life in One Day

How to fix your entire life in one day with 6 executable skills that help you truly change your life rather than giving up in two weeks.

## Quick Start

### What skills do you have?

| Skill | Purpose |
|-------|---------|
| [identity-level](skills/identity-level.md) | Shift from "action level" to "identity level" to solve rebound problems |
| [anti-vision](skills/anti-vision.md) | Use "fear of loss" to drive action, solving the problem of not knowing what you want |
| [mind-stage](skills/mind-stage.md) | Locate your mental development stage, solving the confusing "who am I" problem |
| [cybernetics](skills/cybernetics.md) | Establish feedback iteration system, solving the problem of not doing well consistently |

### Quickly Determine Which to Use

```
Don't know what you want → anti-vision
Working hard but no results → identity-level + cybernetics
Want complete change → one-day-reset
Can't persist → gamification
Confused about who you are → mind-stage
```

## Usage Examples

### Example 1: Weight Loss Always Rebounds

```
User: "I want to lose weight, but I always can't stick with it"

Analysis: "Can't stick with it" → identity-level

1. Ask: If you wanted to lose weight successfully, what kind of person should you become?
    → "I am the kind of person who eats healthily"

2. Ask: If you were already this kind of person, what would you naturally do this week?
    → "Seeing junk food makes me not want to eat it" (not forcing yourself)

3. Establish identity protection:
    → When you want to eat junk food, you're not in a state of "persisting" or "giving up"
      but choosing "who you are"
```

### Example 2: Giving Up on Learning English Halfway

```
User: "I want to learn English, but I always give up halfway"

1. Diagnosis: Only focusing on "learning English" (action), never thinking about "becoming what kind of person" (identity)

2. Establish identity:
    → "I am the kind of person who can think in English" (not "I need to memorize vocabulary")

3. Build anti-vision:
    → Worst-case English scenario in 5 years: Afraid to speak abroad, missing good opportunities
    → One sentence: "I absolutely will not allow myself to become someone who dares not speak"

4. Feedback system:
    → Goal: Able to speak English
    → Action: Practice 30 minutes daily
    → Perception: Recording comparisons, language partner feedback
    → Iteration: Adjust methods

5. Gamification:
    → One-year goal: Daily communication (main quest)
    → One-month project: 100 daily English sentences (leveling up)
    → Daily action: Morning reading 30 minutes (daily task)
```

## Core Methodology

### Identity Level Model

Behavior change exists on two levels:

- **Second Level (Action)**: Forcing yourself to do things ("I need to be more disciplined this year"). Short-term effective, quickly rebounds.
- **First Level (Identity)**: First change "who I am". Identity change naturally brings action change.

### Anti-Vision Construction

People's judgment of "what they don't want" is clearer than "what they want". Use negative energy to drive action:

1. Describe the worst Tuesday 5 years from now
2. Describe missed opportunities 10 years from now
3. End-of-life perspective cost
4. One-sentence anti-vision compression

### Cybernetics Feedback System

Intelligent system's feedback loop:

```
Goal → Action → Perception → Comparison → Iteration
```

High intelligence = ability to learn from this loop and persist.
Low intelligence = stuck on problems, giving up when encountering obstacles.
