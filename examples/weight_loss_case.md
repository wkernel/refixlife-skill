# Case: Weight Loss Always Rebounds

## Problem Description
User: "I want to lose weight, but I always can't stick with it, lose weight then gain it back"

## Diagnosis Process
Keyword: "Can't stick with it" → triggers identity-level skill

## Solution Steps

### Step 1: Confirm Action Level vs Identity Level
**Coach asks**: Are you talking about "what to do" or "who to become"?
**User answers**: I want to lose 20 pounds (action level)

### Step 2: Guide Identity Transformation
**Coach asks**: If you want to achieve this goal, what kind of person should you become?
**User answers**: I should be a disciplined person

**Coach guides**: More specifically, what kind of eating habits person are you?
**User answers**: I am the kind of person who eats healthily (identity statement)

### Step 3: Verify Identity-Natural Behavior
**Coach asks**: If you were already "the kind of person who eats healthily", what would you naturally do this week?
**User answers**:
- Seeing cake, no need to hesitate, just don't eat it
- When eating out, naturally choose vegetable-rich options
- At the supermarket, don't buy junk food to bring home

**Conclusion**: These behaviors are natural, don't require willpower to force ✓

### Step 4: Establish Identity Protection Mechanism
**Coach reminds**: When you want to eat junk food, you're not considering "should I persist?", but asking "am I the kind of person who eats healthily?"

### Step 5: Combine with Other Skills (Optional)
- If still lacking motivation → use anti-vision to imagine yourself continuing to gain weight in 5 years
- If need feedback → use cybernetics to establish weekly weight measurement + diet recording
- If finding it too boring → use gamification to set weekly achievement rewards

## Results
User reports: Starting from the second week, no need to rely on willpower, food choices become natural, lost 8 pounds in a month with no rebound tendency.

## Key Insight
It's not that you "lack willpower", but that you're putting effort on the wrong level (action). Real change happens at the identity level: first become that kind of person, behavior naturally follows.
