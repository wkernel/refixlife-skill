# Case: Giving Up on Learning English Halfway

## Problem Description
User: "I want to learn English, but I always give up halfway, bought many courses but didn't persist"

## Diagnosis Process
Keywords: "Give up halfway" → possibly involves multiple skills
- First check identity: User only talks about "learning English" (action) without mentioning "becoming what kind of person"
- Then check motivation: Possibly lacking anti-vision drive
- Then check feedback: Possibly unaware of own progress
- Then check fun factor: Possibly finds it too boring

## Solution Steps

### Step 1: Establish Identity Level
**Coach asks**: Why do you want to learn English?
**User answers**: To understand American dramas, for work promotion, to travel abroad without embarrassment

**Coach guides**: If your English were excellent, what kind of person should you become?
**User answers**: I should be someone who can think in English
(Identity statement: I am someone who can think in English)

### Step 2: Build Anti-Vision (Increase Motivation)
**Coach asks**: If your English remains at this level in 5 years, what's the worst-case scenario?
**User answers**:
- Traveling abroad completely reliant on tour guides, afraid to order food myself
- Work meetings completely incomprehensible, can only nod along
- Missing many international opportunities, watching colleagues get promoted

**Coach asks**: Use one sentence to summarize what you absolutely refuse to let yourself become?
**User answers**: I absolutely refuse to let myself become a mute person in important situations

### Step 3: Establish Feedback System (Know Your Progress)
**Coach asks**: How do you currently know your English has improved?
**User answers**: I don't know, only rely on feeling

**Coach suggests**:
- Goal: Understand a comedy show's first episode without subtitles
- Action: Daily listening + imitation for 30 minutes
- Perception: Weekly comparison with original text to see comprehension percentage
- Comparison: Record comprehension weekly, goal from 30% to 80%
- Iteration: If a method doesn't work, switch resources or methods

### Step 4: Gamification (Increase Fun and Persistence)
**Coach asks**: How to make this process as addictive as playing a game?
**User answers**: Set levels and rewards

**Co-design together**:
- One-year goal (main quest): Able to watch dramas smoothly without subtitles → unlock "Drama Master" title
- One-month project (leveling up): Master 100 daily English sentences this month → level up every 20 sentences completed
- Daily action (daily task): Morning practice 30 minutes → earn 100 experience points daily when completed
- Bottom-line constraint (game rules): Even when busy, ensure 5 minutes uninterrupted

### Step 5: Execution and Review
**User execution feedback**:
- First week: Identity establishment completed, started daily morning practice
- Second week: Through audio comparison discovered pronunciation issues, adjusted method (read-aloud + correction)
- Third week: Achieved first micro-goal: able to understand 50% of simple conversations
- Fourth week: Persistence rate reached 90%, started developing language sense

## Results
User reports: After two months able to understand simple dramas, after three months capable of basic communication, after six months English work reports no pressure.

## Key Insight
Learning English isn't about "memorizing vocabulary lists" (action), but about becoming "someone who can think in English" (identity).
Motivation comes from fear of losing opportunities (anti-vision), persistence relies on gamification and timely feedback (feedback system + gamification).
